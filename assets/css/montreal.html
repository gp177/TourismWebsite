Montreal

Montréal (French pronunciation: [mɔ̃ʁeal]), is the most populous municipality in the Canadian province of Quebec and the 2nd-most populous in Canada, after Toronto. Originally called Ville-Marie, or "City of Mary,"[8] it is believed to be named after Mount Royal, though there is some debate about this. The city has a distinct four-season continental climate, with warm-to-hot summers and cold, snowy winters.[9] In 2016, Montreal had a population of 1,704,694.[2] Montreal's metropolitan area had a population of 4,098,927[4] and a population of 1,958,257 in the urban agglomeration, with all of the municipalities on the Island of Montreal included.[10] Legally a French-speaking city,[11] 60.5% of Montrealers speak French at home, 21.2% speak English and 19.8% speak neither.[12] Montreal is one of the most bilingual cities in Quebec and Canada,[citation needed] with 56% of the population able to speak both official languages.[13] Montreal is the second-largest primarily French-speaking city in the world after Paris.[14][15][16][17]
